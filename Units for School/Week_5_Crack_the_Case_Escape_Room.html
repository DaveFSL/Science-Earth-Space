Evidence File 1 — The Pull of the Sun

LOCKED

What two things must be balanced to keep Earth in orbit?

Earn letter 1 of 5

Remember the Orbit Lab: too slow and gravity wins (crash), too fast and motion wins (escape). It's a balance of gravity and motion.

In the Orbit Lab, you turned gravity OFF while Earth orbited. What happened?

Earn letter 2 of 5

With nothing pulling it, an object travels in a straight line. The orbit only curves because gravity bends Earth's path.

Which list is in the correct order outwards from the Sun?

Earn letter 3 of 5

Use your mnemonic! My Very Easy Method… Mercury is closest to the Sun.

Why is Mercury much hotter than Neptune?

Earn letter 4 of 5

Think about your pocket solar system — the inner planets are crammed near the Sun, the outer ones are a long way out.

What is an orbit?

Earn letter 5 of 5

Careful — spinning on an axis is rotation. An orbit is the path around something (a revolution).

🔓 FILE 1 UNLOCKED

Gravity and motion together keep the planets in orbit. Code word earned: MOTION

Evidence File 2 — Why Darkness Falls

LOCKED

What causes day and night?

Earn letter 1 of 5

Big misconception alert! The Sun isn't moving around us — we are spinning. Your Station B globe-and-torch model proved it.

How long does Earth take to rotate once?

Earn letter 2 of 5

One rotation = one day = 24 hours. The 365-day one is the revolution around the Sun. Don't mix them up!

It's the middle of the day here. What's happening on the opposite side of Earth?

Earn letter 3 of 5

Half of Earth faces the Sun (day), half faces away (night). The far side is in the opposite — midnight.

The Sun appears to rise in the east. Why?

Earn letter 4 of 5

The Sun only appears to move. It's Earth turning west-to-east that brings the Sun into view in the east.

What's the difference between rotate and revolve?

Earn letter 5 of 5

The golden question: moving around itself (rotate) or around something else (revolve)?

🔓 FILE 2 UNLOCKED

Day and night come from Earth turning on its axis. Code word earned: AXIS

Evidence File 3 — The Secret of the Seasons

LOCKED

What really causes the seasons?

Earn letter 1 of 5

The biggest myth in the unit! It's NOT distance — Earth is actually closest to the Sun in January. It's the 23.5° tilt.

When it's summer in Australia, what season is it in the UK?

Earn letter 2 of 5

The hemispheres lean opposite ways. When the south leans towards the Sun (our summer), the north leans away (their winter).

As Earth orbits the Sun all year, what does its tilted axis do?

Earn letter 3 of 5

The Season Machine showed this: the axis never changes direction. It's Earth's position on the orbit that changes which hemisphere leans sunward.

In the Season Machine, you turned the tilt OFF. What happened to daylight hours?

Earn letter 4 of 5

No tilt = every day about 12 hours = no seasons. The tilt is the whole reason seasons exist.

Why do places near the poles (like Helsinki) have huge daylight changes through the year?

Earn letter 5 of 5

Your Graph Lab showed it: Helsinki's line swung far more than ours. The closer to the poles, the bigger the daylight swing.

🔓 FILE 3 UNLOCKED

The tilt gives the year its rhythm of seasons. Code word earned: RHYTHM

Evidence File 4 — A Detective's Tools

LOCKED

Your pocket solar system was great at showing distances. What did it get WRONG?

Earn letter 1 of 5

Every model shows some things well and others badly. The strip nailed distance but couldn't show true size or movement.

Your Monday shadow photos got shorter each week. What's that evidence of?

Earn letter 2 of 5

Same stick, same time, shorter shadow = the Sun is higher in our sky each week. That's the tilt + revolution at work.

Why do scientists use models (like globes, diagrams and simulators)?

Earn letter 3 of 5

A model is a thinking tool — it makes something too big, too far or too slow into something we can see and test.

A good detective relies on…

Earn letter 4 of 5

Claims need evidence. That's been our motto since Week 1 — it's also exactly what your assessment rewards.

Why is it useful to compare your shadow data with another group's?

Earn letter 5 of 5

Scientists check each other's results to find errors and build trust in the evidence (that's AC9S6I05 — comparing methods and findings).

🔓 FILE 4 UNLOCKED

Evidence and models are the tools of science. Code word earned: SCIENCE
