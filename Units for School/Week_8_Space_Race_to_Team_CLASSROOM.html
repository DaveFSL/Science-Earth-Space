Did you know?

Australia and the Moon landing 🇦🇺

When Neil Armstrong stepped onto the Moon in 1969, the clearest TV pictures were received by radio dishes in New South Wales — the Parkes telescope and the Honeysuckle Creek tracking station near Canberra. As Earth turned, Australia was facing the Moon at exactly the right moment, so our dishes relayed that famous footage to about 600 million people. The whole planet watched the Moon landing partly through Australian eyes.

Explicit teaching

From a race to a team — and a mess to clean up

🏁 The Space Race (1950s–60s)

The space race was a competition between the USA and the Soviet Union. Competition drove fast progress: the first satellite, first person in space, first Moon landing — all within about 12 years.

🤝 The Space Team (today)

Today the biggest projects are collaborations. The International Space Station was built by 15+ countries; the James Webb Space Telescope is NASA + Europe (ESA) + Canada (CSA). Sharing knowledge gets us further than any country could alone.

♻ A shared problem: space junk

Sixty years of launches have left millions of pieces of debris orbiting Earth — dead satellites, rocket parts, even flecks of paint, all moving fast enough to be dangerous. No single country can fix it: keeping space usable is a shared responsibility, like looking after any shared environment. (Missions like DART even test how we might protect Earth from asteroids — planetary defence is a team sport too.)

Many hands

It took all kinds of people

Space science isn't just famous astronauts. It took mathematicians, engineers and scientists from many countries and backgrounds — and plenty were left out of the story for too long.

🧮
Katherine Johnson (USA)
Mathematician whose calculations put the first Americans safely into orbit and on the path to the Moon.
🚀
Valentina Tereshkova (Soviet Union)
The first woman in space, in 1963 — decades before many countries sent any women at all.
🛰
Andy Thomas (Australia)
Adelaide-born astronaut who flew on the Space Shuttle and lived aboard the Mir space station and the ISS.
📡
The Parkes & Honeysuckle Creek teams (Australia)
The engineers who relayed the Moon-landing broadcast to the world.
